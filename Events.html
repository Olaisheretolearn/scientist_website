Saturday 17 Event

First Year guide

In-Person

So you got accepted… congrats 🎉
Now what? Classes, profs, timetables, trying to find where the heck “3L04” even is, and pretending you totally aren’t lost in Centennial Hall for the 5th time this week.
Our First Year Guide is your cheat-sheet to surviving (and actually enjoying) your first semester. We’ll cover the basics (like how not to starve on cafeteria fries alone)
and the bigger stuff (study hacks, finding your people, and why joining a student society isn’t just free pizza — although, yes, there’s pizza 🍕). It’s part survival manual, part inside-jokes, and all the stuff we wish someone had told us on day one. By the end, you’ll be walking around like a pro — or at least like someone who knows which staircases don’t lead to a dead end.

October 2025

Saturday 17 Event

May Professional Development Workshop

In-Person

“Oh… I have no job experience, what do I even put in my resume?”
We hear that all the time. And that’s exactly why we’re hosting this workshop 👩🏾‍💻👨🏽‍💼.
Turns out, you don’t need a fancy internship at GenomeCanada to have a solid CV. Your projects, classwork, group assignments, volunteer gigs, club involvement, even that time you organized study sessions for your friends — they all count (and we’ll show you how to make them sparkle ✨).
At our Resume Build Workshop, we’ll walk you through how to spin your experiences into recruiter-friendly bullet points,
what sections to include when you feel like you’ve got “nothing,” and the small formatting hacks that make a huge difference. Bring your laptop, a draft (even if it’s just your name at the top), and we’ll help you leave with something that actually screams: “Hire me, I’ve got potential.”
